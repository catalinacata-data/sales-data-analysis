# Superstore Sales Analysis

## Project Overview
This project explores retail sales data to evaluate regional performance, profitability, and the impact of discount strategies on business margins. The objective is to identify inefficiencies and provide strategic recommendations based on data-driven insights.

---

## Dataset
- 51,290 transactions
- 27 variables including Sales, Profit, Discount, Region, Category and Shipping Cost
- No missing values detected

---

## Key Analyses

### 1. Regional Performance
- Central generates the highest total sales and overall profit.
- Canada shows the highest profit margin despite low sales volume.

### 2. Profitability Analysis
- Profit margin varies significantly across regions.
- High-volume regions are not always the most efficient.

### 3. Discount Impact
- Profitability declines sharply as discount levels increase.
- Discounts above 30% consistently generate negative average profit.
- Southeast Asia applies the highest average discount (~27%) and has one of the lowest profit margins (~2%).

---

## Key Insights

- Aggressive discounting strategies are strongly associated with margin erosion.
- Southeast Asia’s low profitability appears driven by excessive discounting.
- Canada presents a high-efficiency growth opportunity.
- Discount thresholds should be monitored to avoid sustained losses.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Reassess pricing and discount policies in Southeast Asia.
- Implement discount caps (≤30%) to protect margins.
- Explore expansion opportunities in high-margin regions.
- Continuously monitor regional profit margins to guide strategy.
